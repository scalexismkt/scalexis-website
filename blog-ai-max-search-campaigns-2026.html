AI Max for Search Campaigns Is Coming for Your DSA Setup. Fix This First.

AK Ajinkya Khadse 02.07.2026

AI Max for Search campaigns is not just another Google Ads toggle. For a lot of lead generation accounts, it is the point where weak conversion tracking, messy landing pages, and old Manual CPC habits start getting expensive faster.

Primary keyword: AI Max for Search campaigns

Search intent: founders and paid media teams comparing the DSA transition, migration risk, and setup changes before auto-upgrades.

Key takeaways

  • Google announced on April 15, 2026 that AI Max was moving out of beta, then updated the timeline on June 11, 2026 so DSA sunset and auto-upgrade begin in February 2027. Campaigns using automatically created assets and campaign-level broad match still start auto-upgrading in September 2026.
  • AI Max for Search campaigns expands reach with search term matching, text customization, and final URL expansion. That means the system learns from your current keywords, ads, URLs, and conversion signals, not from some magical separate AI setup.
  • Google's own FAQ says Manual CPC limits AI Max because search term matching does not work with manual bidding. If you still rely on Manual CPC, you are not testing the product Google is asking you to migrate into.
  • The real migration risk is not the feature name. It is bad inputs: weak lead goals, poor page controls, unclear brand rules, and no benchmark before Google changes legacy settings for you.

The update most advertisers will misread

A lot of people saw "DSA is moving to AI Max" and assumed Google was just renaming an old feature. That is too simple. On April 15, 2026, Google said eligible campaigns using Dynamic Search Ads, automatically created assets, and campaign-level broad match would start moving into AI Max. Then Google revised the DSA part on June 11, 2026: DSA sunset and auto-upgrade now begin in February 2027, while the other two legacy settings still move starting in September 2026.

That date change matters because it gives advertisers a short window to clean up the account before migration pressure gets real. It also tells you what Google thinks the future of Search looks like: less manual maintenance, more AI-led matching, more landing-page expansion, and more dependence on the quality of the signals you send back into the platform.

Why lead generation accounts are more exposed than they think

Ecommerce accounts usually have clearer conversion signals. A purchase is a purchase. Lead generation is messier. Many accounts still optimize to any form fill, any call click, or any low-friction event that looks cheap in the platform but never turns into revenue. When AI Max for Search campaigns starts expanding query reach and picking landing pages, that weak signal becomes the instruction set.

That is why this change is bigger for service brands than it looks on paper. If your Google Ads account cannot tell the difference between a qualified lead and a bad inquiry, AI Max will still optimize. It will just optimize toward the wrong thing. That problem does not start when the auto-upgrade lands. It is already sitting in the account.

What to fix before AI Max expands your reach

1. Clean up your conversion actions first

Before you touch the migration, audit which conversions are included in bidding. Remove weak goals that make the account look busy but do not reflect sales quality. If you can import offline stages, booked consultations, or CRM-qualified leads, do it now. Google's May 20, 2026 bidding update also points toward journey-aware bidding for better lead-quality signals. That direction is obvious: the more your account can separate a junk lead from a real opportunity, the better automation has a chance of helping.

2. Decide which pages AI Max should be allowed to expand into

AI Max uses final URL expansion and learns from your website content. That is useful when your page inventory is clean. It is risky when the site includes thin pages, outdated offers, old campaign pages, or blog posts that answer the wrong intent. Review the URLs you actually want paid traffic to hit. Tighten page controls before the platform makes broader matching feel normal.

This is where a lot of smaller accounts get lazy. They assume better targeting will compensate for bad landing-page discipline. It will not. If the site sends mixed signals, the campaign learns mixed signals. That is the same reason we keep pushing teams to connect paid media, SEO, and page quality instead of treating them like separate jobs. If you need that broader cleanup, start with your paid ads and performance marketing setup, not just the AI Max toggle.

3. Stop judging the migration through a Manual CPC mindset

Google's FAQ is blunt here: if you use Manual CPC, the full benefits of AI Max are limited because search term matching does not work with manual bidding. That does not mean every account should blindly switch bidding tomorrow. It means you should stop pretending Manual CPC is a fair baseline for an AI-led product that depends on automated bidding signals.

A better test is to move a clean campaign onto conversion-based bidding, protect it with sane budgets and business rules, and compare outcomes against a stable benchmark. If you are not ready to trust automated bidding with your current conversion setup, that is the warning sign. The account probably is not ready for AI Max either.

4. Put guardrails around text and brand controls

AI Max does not only broaden matching. It also uses text customization. Google positions that as a relevance gain, and in some accounts it will be. But relevance is only useful if the messaging stays inside your offer, brand tone, and funnel reality. Review text guidelines, brand controls, location controls, and the claims your landing pages can actually support.

This is the part most teams underestimate because it sounds cosmetic. It is not. If AI-generated or AI-customized copy starts promising the wrong thing, you do not just lose CTR quality. You create bad leads, worse qualification calls, and reporting noise that makes the campaign look better than the pipeline feels. We covered the broader version of that problem in our guide to AI paid ads strategy. AI Max makes the same issue more specific and more urgent.

5. Benchmark before Google benchmarks it for you

Google recommends upgrading early and using one-click experiments. That advice is self-serving, but it is also correct. If you wait for forced migration, you lose the clean before-and-after comparison that tells you whether the account improved because of AI Max or because the baseline was already broken.

Pick a campaign with decent tracking, controlled budgets, and clear landing pages. Record its current query quality, qualified lead rate, cost per qualified lead, close rate if available, and the pages that actually convert. Then test. If you only compare clicks, CTR, and platform conversion counts, you are not measuring the change that matters.

Where Google is taking Search next

The migration story makes more sense when you look at Google's broader May 20, 2026 announcements. Google said new Gemini-built ad formats are coming to Search, and it also previewed new bidding and budgeting tools such as journey-aware bidding, Smart Bidding Exploration, and demand-led pacing. The direction is obvious: Search ads are moving toward broader intent interpretation, more automation, and more dependence on first-party business signals.

That is why this article is not really about one feature. It is about whether your account is built for the version of Google Ads that is already arriving. If your measurement is weak now, more AI will not rescue it. It will just scale your ambiguity.

A practical AI Max migration checklist

  1. List every conversion action currently included in bidding and remove weak goals.
  2. Import qualified lead stages, offline conversion value, or booked-call outcomes where possible.
  3. Audit the landing pages you are comfortable letting final URL expansion use.
  4. Review brand controls, location controls, text guidelines, and negative keyword coverage.
  5. Test AI Max on conversion-based bidding, not on a Manual CPC fallback that disables core matching behavior.
  6. Take a benchmark snapshot before any voluntary upgrade or automatic migration.
  7. Track query quality, qualified lead rate, close rate, and revenue alongside platform conversions.
  8. Keep GA4 and CRM measurement aligned so reporting does not drift after the campaign expands reach.

Frequently asked questions

When does Google upgrade Dynamic Search Ads to AI Max?

Google first announced the change on April 15, 2026. It then updated the timeline on June 11, 2026 so DSA sunset and auto-upgrade begin in February 2027. Campaigns using automatically created assets and campaign-level broad match still begin auto-upgrading in September 2026.

Should lead generation advertisers use AI Max for Search campaigns?

Yes, but only after the account can distinguish a qualified lead from a cheap one. AI Max can help expand reach and improve relevance, but weak conversion signals make the system optimize toward bad outcomes faster.

What should I measure during an AI Max test?

Measure more than clicks and platform conversions. Watch search-term quality, landing-page quality, qualified lead rate, cost per qualified lead, close rate, and revenue or pipeline value. If you already track AI referrals in analytics, keep that reporting clean too. Our recent piece on tracking AI traffic in GA4 covers the measurement discipline that paid teams are going to need more often, not less.

Final takeaway

AI Max for Search campaigns is not the scary part. Blind migration is. The accounts that handle this well will be the ones with clean conversion signals, disciplined landing pages, realistic bidding strategy, and a benchmark taken before Google changes the furniture for them.
